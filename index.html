A Brief History of NodeBots

Author's Note: This was written at 3AM. I will be making edits as necessary. Please feel free to file any errors or missing data as GitHub issues, and I will see to them as expeditiously as I can.

NodeBots history is naturally very brief, but still not a story that's shared relatively often.

The beginning

It all started with a small, but important node module: node-serialport. What this module did was allow node applications to communicate with the serial port on your computer. In most modern computers, this means communicating via the USB port.

This module went relaively unnoticed to all but the experienced robotics hobbyist- most of us, including the author, had no idea what the module truly unlocked.

Johnny-Five

This is the part of NodeBots history that most of us remember- the creation of Johnny-Five. What this library did was put a simple-to-use javascript API around the Arduino's Firmata firmware.

Suddenly, robotics was accessible to JS programmers everywhere- install this npm module, copy the code from the litany of well-maintained docs hosted in johnny-five's repository, and you were off!

As the number of devices working with Arduino began to grow, so did the popularity of JS robotics- the first NodeBots Day in 2013 was a rousing success in many countries, and at JSConf, dozens of attendees happily hacked away on their first Arduino projects (this author included!)

The explosion

Shortly thereafter was the beginning of what I lovingly call 'the cambrian explosion of NdoeBots.'

As Arduino became ever-more popular with JS devs thanks to Johnny-Five, more platforms began to show promise with the use of javascript. Parrot Drones and Spheros were some of the first to break from johnny-five- but because they were all Node modules, many used them togther. Gamepads suddenly had modules on npm, and you could control your nodecopter, carrying an arduino, with your XBox controller.

Raspberry Pi, BeagleBone Black, and dedicated, ARM-based mini-computers encouraged even further NodeBots exploration: I think a million methophorical lightbulbs switched on when someone discovered you could plug an Arduino into a Raspberry Pi and run johnny-five- severing one of the very few limitations that tethering created.

"Native" NodeBots

Not long after this, platforms that didn't require a wrapper- that ran JS natively- began to surface. Kickstarters for the Espruino and Tessel emerged, and quickly became wildly succesful. These two platforms are now widely available.

Today

Today, a litany of platforms exist that can be controlled with javascript- and this site was established to help make sense of them all.
